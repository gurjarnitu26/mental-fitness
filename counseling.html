Professional Counseling & Support

Find the right mental health professional and support resources to help you on your journey to wellness.

Types of Mental Health Professionals

Psychiatrists

Medical doctors who can diagnose mental health conditions and prescribe medication. They often work with therapists to provide comprehensive treatment.

Psychologists

Licensed professionals who provide therapy and psychological assessments. They use various therapeutic approaches to help with mental health issues.

Licensed Counselors

Mental health counselors who provide talk therapy and support. They help individuals work through personal challenges and develop coping strategies.

Social Workers

Licensed clinical social workers who provide therapy and connect you with community resources. They often focus on social and environmental factors.

24/7 Crisis Support

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline

988

Available 24/7 for crisis support and suicide prevention

Crisis Text Line

Text HOME to 741741

Free, 24/7 crisis support via text message

SAMHSA Helpline

1-800-662-4357

Treatment referral and information service

Finding the Right Therapist

Ask for Referrals

Start with your primary care doctor, friends, or family members who have had positive therapy experiences.

Check Your Insurance

Contact your insurance provider to see which mental health professionals are covered under your plan.

Consider Specialties

Look for therapists who specialize in your specific concerns, such as anxiety, depression, or trauma.

Schedule Consultations

Many therapists offer brief consultations to help you determine if they're a good fit for your needs.
