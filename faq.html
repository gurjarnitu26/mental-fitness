Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about mental health, treatment options, and getting help.

What should I do if I'm having a mental health crisis?

If you're experiencing a mental health emergency, call 988 for the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline, text HOME to 741741 for the Crisis Text Line, or go to your nearest emergency room. These services are available 24/7 and provide immediate support. Don't hesitate to reach out - your safety and well-being are the top priority.

How do I find a mental health professional?

Start by contacting your healthcare provider for referrals, check with your insurance for covered providers, or use online directories like Psychology Today. Many community health centers also offer mental health services on a sliding fee scale. You can also ask friends or family members for recommendations if they've had positive experiences with mental health professionals.

What's the difference between therapy and counseling?

While often used interchangeably, therapy typically refers to longer-term treatment for mental health conditions, while counseling may focus on specific issues or life transitions. Both can be effective depending on your needs. The most important factor is finding a qualified professional you feel comfortable with and who uses evidence-based approaches.

How can I support someone with mental health challenges?

Listen without judgment, educate yourself about their condition, offer practical support, encourage professional help when appropriate, and take care of your own mental health too. Small gestures of understanding can make a big difference. Remember that you don't need to have all the answers - sometimes just being present and supportive is enough.

Do I need medication for mental health treatment?

Medication is not always necessary for mental health treatment. Many people benefit from therapy alone, while others may need a combination of therapy and medication. A mental health professional can help determine the best treatment approach for your specific situation. The decision should be made collaboratively between you and your healthcare provider.

How long does mental health treatment take?

The duration of treatment varies greatly depending on the individual, the type of condition, and the treatment approach. Some people see improvement in a few sessions, while others may need longer-term support. Your therapist will work with you to set realistic expectations and regularly assess progress toward your goals.

How much does mental health treatment cost?

Costs vary widely depending on your insurance, location, and type of provider. Many therapists offer sliding scale fees, and community health centers often provide low-cost or free services. Some employers also offer Employee Assistance Programs (EAPs) with free counseling sessions. Don't let cost prevent you from seeking help - there are resources available for every budget.

Is mental health treatment confidential?

Yes, mental health treatment is confidential. Mental health professionals are bound by strict confidentiality laws and ethical codes. They cannot share your information without your written consent, except in specific situations involving safety concerns. Your privacy and confidentiality are fundamental aspects of effective mental health care.
